Epidemiological methods in health research

Epidemiology is the foundation of population and public health sciences.Its contribution to detection and prevention of diseases and promotion of health cannot be over-estimated. Epidemiology has developed into a science with its own methods and tools to achieve success. However, often many researchers in the health sciences run into varied challenges because of deficit in grasping the methods of epidemiology and application of these in their research work. New challenges and concepts emerge during the research process. Finding the solutions to the encountered problems is not always hands-on. Issues concerning decision on the right study design, right statistical techniques, and dealing with biases in analysis are a few of occurring dilemma. This course takes participants through the science of epidemiology. At the end of the course, participants will be stimulated into deeper appreciation and grasp of epidemiologic methods in the scientific research process. Teaching of the course includes seminars, workshops, and analysis of specific cases, with the aim of providing participants with the skill-sets required to deal with similar situations in their own research domains. Issues covered in the course include.

    • Choice of the right epidemiologic study design for a study
    • Measures of disease burden and associations in epidemiology
    • Establishing causal inference in epidemiology
    • Choice of the appropriate statistical techniques for my data types
    • Step-by-step fundamentals of data analysis in epidemiology
    • Keys to robust data presentation in manuscripts and conferences
    • Dealing with issues of bias and confounding at study design and analysis stages
Reproductivity research

During the last half century, many misconducts have been documented in scientific research: several published papers have been retracted as a result of outright fraud, data manipulation, data theft, and other negative practices. The core tenets of the scientific research processes are not always upheld by many researchers. However, during the past decade, the field of reproducible research has been gaining popularity and is being embraced across many scientific research fields. Reproductivity refers to the ability of an investigator to duplicate the results of a prior study using the same methods as were used by the original investigator. Its overarching goal is to revive excellence in scientific research. Reproductivity requires specific tools and skill-sets at each stage of the research process, but these are not widely known by many researchers. The need to continue to teach the benefits and processes of Reproductivity in science is fundamental. This course covers the landscape of Reproductivity in scientific research, focusing particularly on issues of Reproductivity in the medical field. The course provides participants with the practical tools and algorithms required for the implementation of Reproductivity in their own research work. Several case studies are analyzed in order to appreciate the various aspects of the field of reproducible research. Tutoring is participatory, with hands-on practical sessions that will allow participants to implement the skill-sets learned. The course is suitable for postgraduate students, junior and senior researchers from any field of research. The course covers the following main themes.

• Fundamental principles of scientific research and its benefits to the society

    • Key and related concept in Reproductivity of scientific research
    • Causes of irreproducible scientific research
    • Case studies of irreproducible research
    • Rationale, benefits, and challenges of reproducible research
    • Global best practices on reproducible research
    • Key steps and tools to implement Reproductivity
    • Computational algorithms to reproducible research
Data analysis and software

The world and everyday activities we engaged in have become ongoing data repository that benefit scientific research. Research data are emanating from different angles, increasing in both depth and complexity. Analysis of research data to make sense of them and inform key decisions for the society, for medical care, and for policy, can be perplexing for many researchers. At the same time, the computer software to analyze data have also grown in number, and choosing relevant software to analyze data can be challenging for many researchers. This course takes participants step-by-step through the process of planning and analyzing their research-generated data and interpreting results emanating from their analyses. During the course, a couple of software will be introduced to demonstrate how the different data analyses can be implemented across software. Tutoring is hands-on, allowing participants to implement the skill-sets learned. The course is suitable for postgraduate students, junior and senior researchers from any field of research.

Successful academic publishing

Academic publishing remains the bedrock for assessing scientific productivity. In recent decades, the pressure to publish has led to production of poor scientific papers that do not provide meaningful contribution to growth of knowledge. The exponential growth in academic journals, many of which are ‘predatory’ in that their only motivation is to make money, has led to lousy publication of scientific works. Such journals end up publishing almost anything submitted to them, without the usual careful checks and balances and critical peer reviewing processes that have been the foundation of the scientific process. Many academicians, given the pressure to publish, have usually fallen victim of this below par scientific publishing. Adding to the challenges is that many scientists have not learned the overarching goal of academic publishing and the processes and strategies to achieve success. Academic publishing requires certain skills that every scientist needs to learn. Unfortunately, such skills have usually not been part of the mainstream curricula training of scientists. Many scientists then end up growing in their publishing career through trial and error or through collaborative networks, which usually are not available to all researchers. This course takes participants into the world of academic publishing, focusing primarily on journal publishing. The course covers issues around: thinking of a scientific paper, developing the structure of a scientific paper, writing a scientific paper, going through the publication process of a scientific paper, and dealing with reviewers and editorial criticisms of the scientific paper. The overarching goal of the course is to help participants become successful in their scientific publishing career, ensuring that they communicate and disseminate their scientific findings in a way that contributes to the growth of knowledge and gain positive impact on the society. The course is suitable for postgraduate students, junior and senior researchers from any field of research.

How to secure research grants

Scientific Research majorly depends on financial contributions from various institutions in the society, including governmental institutions, non-governmental non-for-profit organizations, and private companies, which come in the form of grants to scientists. Without these contributions to scientific research, the success of science will be deficient. Usually, scientists have to apply and compete to receive these funds in order to perform their scientific works. While many scientists have been successful in obtaining research grants, thus achieving impressive milestones in their careers, others have been less successful. As with success in scientific publishing, success with securing research grants is an important aspect of a successful scientific career. However, for many scientists, especially those who are young in their scientific career, knowing how to be successful in securing funding for their research projects remains challenging. Often the questions are asked: what are the sources of grants to apply?; how do I write a successful grant application? This course answers these questions and many more and provides participants with the tools required to identify the appropriate sources to apply for research grants. Importantly, regardless of the grant source, the course guides participants through writing a successful grant application. The course is suitable for postgraduate students, junior and senior researchers from any field of research.
